Keynote Speakers

Sandra Begay-Campell



Sandra Begay-Campbell is a Principal Member of the Technical Staff at Sandia National Laboratories and leads Sandia’s technical efforts to assist American Indian tribes with their renewable energy developments. Sandra is also a former Regent (Trustee) for the University of New Mexico.

Sandra received a Bachelor of Science - Civil Engineering degree from the University of New Mexico. She worked at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratories before she earned a Master of Science - Structural Engineering degree from Stanford University.

Sandra is a recipient of the American Indian Science and Engineering Society’s Life-time Achievement Award; the University of New Mexico’s 2007 Zia Alumnus Award; the 2005 UNM School of Engineering Distinguished Alumnus Award and the Stanford University 2000 Multicultural Alumni of the Year Award. She was also selected as a recipient of the Governor's Award for Outstanding Women from the New Mexico Commission on the Status of Women.

Sandra is recognized in a book profiling women engineers, “Changing Our World: True Stories of Women Engineers” and is included in the chapter "Women in Power", which describes her effort to provide electricity through solar panels and other alternative energy solutions to hundreds of remote tribal members on the Navajo Reservation.

Professor Otakuye Conroy-Ben



Dr. Otakuye Conroy-Ben is an Assistant Professor at the University of Utah, in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering. She is originally from the Pine Ridge Indian Reservation in South Dakota. Dr. Conroy-Ben received a B.S. in Chemistry from the University of Notre Dame, and a M.A. in Analytical Chemistry, M.S. in Environmental Engineering, and Ph.D. in Chemical and Environmental Engineering all from the University of Arizona. She completed a postdoc appointment in biochemistry in the area of metal resistance in bacteria. She has previously served on the Board of Directors of the American Indian Science and Engineering Society and was a founding member of the Indigenous Women in Science Network. Otakuye appeared in the movies Dances with Wolves and Miracle in the Wilderness, was a former fancy shawl dander, and served as “Miss Black Hills Nation.” She is married to Colin Ben, and has a daughter, Wayuwita Win.


Distinguished Guest Speakers & Workshop Leaders

Ron Gutman



Ron Gutman is an inventor and a serial technology entrepreneur who has started and built several successful companies in digital health. He's currently the Founder and CEO of HealthTap, the fastest growing digital-health startup that is helping many millions everywhere find the best care and make better decisions about their health and well-being. HealthTap has saved numerous lives and is helping everyone everywhere feel good by connecting them with a network of over 67,000 U.S. doctors for answers, tips, checklists, news, and live Virtual Consults. Ron is responsible for the company’s vision, innovation, products, and growth.

Ron is also a writer (he wrote a very popular TED book on the hidden powers of smiling and has contributed articles to Fortune, Forbes, etc.) and is a speaker in health and technology conferences (including TED, Stanford Medicine X, and Health2.0). Ron is also an angel investor and an advisor to health and technology companies.

Professor Juan Santiago



Juan Santiago received his MS and PhD in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 1995. His research includes the development of microsystems for on-chip chemical and biochemical analysis, methods for sample preparation, miniature pumps, and electric-field based deionization methods. Applications of this work include molecular diagnostics, drug discovery, portable power generation, and the production of drinking water.

Among other recognition, he was awarded a National Science Foundation Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE) ('04) and the Outstanding Achievement in Academia Award by the GEM Foundation ('06). He is a Fellow of the American Physical Society, a Fellow of the American Society of Mechanical Engineering, an Associate Editor of the journal Microfluidics and Nanofluidics, co-founder of several companies in the microfluidics area, co-inventor of micron-resolution particle image velocimetry (Micro-PIV), and director of the Stanford Microfluidics Laboratory. He has served as Associate Editor of the journal Lab on a Chip ('08-'13).

Santiago has given 25 keynote and named lectures and more than 100 additional invited lectures. As one measure of impact, his work is cited about 1000 times per year (Google Scholar h index of 57). He has graduated 24 PhD students and advised eight postdoctoral researchers. 16 of his former advisees are now professors at major universities. He has authored and co-authored over 150 archival publications and 200 conference papers, and holds 34 patents.

Professor Phil Grant



Dr. Phil Grant is an Assistant Professor of Medicine within the division of infectious diseases at Stanford University. His research focuses on the antiretroviral therapy and complications of HIV including immune reconstitution inflammatory disease, osteoporosis, and cardiovascular disease. He is originally from the Yurok Reservation in Northern California. After graduating high school, he received a B.A. in Economics from Harvard University before attending medical school at UCSF. He proceeded to complete his residency at the University of Washington.

In addition to his academic duties as a professor at the School of Medicine, Dr. Grant is an advisor to many indigenous undergraduate students at Stanford. In his spare time, he additionally serves as a Head Coach for the American Youth Soccer Organization and as a classroom volunteer for the Menlo Park and Palo Alto School Districts.

Dr. Noé Pablo Lozano



Dr. Noé Pablo Lozano has over twenty years of higher education experiences in academic and student affairs administration, especially in outreach, recruitment, retention, and academic enrichment programs. He lectures on culture and technology, organizational behavior, psychosocial factors, cross-cultural-diversity education, and learning methods. Noe is the Associate Dean for Student Affairs and Director of the Engineering Diversity Programs in the School of Engineering at Stanford University. He oversees dean’s office policies and diversity affairs in the areas of academic assistance programs (SSEA, ACE, Summer Session grants etc.), academic advising, fellowship and assistantship programs, tutorial and learning programs, summer undergraduate research programs, Pre-college Math Institute, Opportunity Job Fair, special co-op programs, and other academic assistance and advising services. His goal is to empower students to succeed through academic development, personal development, professional growth, and achieving individual goals.

He received a Ph.D. with distinction from the University of California, Berkeley in Higher Education, Statistics and Curriculum Development, a Masters from Stanford University in Administrative Policy Analysis, and a teaching credential and Bachelor’s degree with honors in Psychology with a Bio minor from the University of California, Santa Cruz. His research involves educational learning and intervention programs. He is an VPUE advisor of merit, and has received advisor and lecturer of the year awards several times from the Stanford Terman Scholars, AISES, NSBE-SBSE, SHPE-MAES-SSCLES, SWE, and Stanford’s El Centro. He is Stanford University President’s Awardee for Excellence in Diversity.

LCDR Clark Petri

LCDR Petri originally enlisted in the Navy in August of 1999 out of Middletown, CA. After bootcamp in Great Lakes, Il, he began his training as a Nuclear Electrician Mate. Successful leadership opportunities through his year and a half of training assisted in his selection as a staff instructor and subsequent selection to a NROTC Scholarship at Oregon State University. While in college LCDR Petri successfully passed the Naval Reactors interviews in Washington DC and was selected into the Nuclear Engineering Officer program as a future Surface Warfare Officer.

Commissioned in June of 2005, then ENS Petri reported to USS Halsey (DDG-97) in San Diego, CA. He was part of the commissioning crew, served as the 1st Lieutenant, took part in Halsey's initial Combat System Ships Qualification Trial and proudly served on her maiden deployment. He then reported to Charleston, SC, to again go through the Navy's nuclear training. After successfully completing training he reported to USS Enterprise (CVN-65) where he served in Machinery Division during Enterprise's Extended Dry-docking Selected Restricted Availability (EDSRA), steam plant test program, sea trials and Operational Reactor Safeguard Exam (ORSE).

After his tour on Enterprise, LCDR Petri reported to COMNAVAIRPAC PSNS where he served as the A-OIC for carrier oversight and taught Officers junior to himself the course work for their final Nuclear Engineer Officer examination. Upon transfer from COMNAVAIRPAC he reported to NRD San Francisco as an Officer Recruiter, Division Officer and the Engineering Programs Coordinator. He was recently recognized as the Officer Recruiter of the Year for FY14.

In his free time LCDR Petri enjoys competing in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, reading, and CrossFit. LCDR Petri’s awards include three Navy Commendation Medals and other unit and campaign awards.

Darrel Aubrey

Darrel Aubrey is a FieldTech Resident (FTR) at Google. Fieldtechs support Google’s technology from corporate infrastructure to end users. He grew up in northern California, is a Karuk tribal member and of Yurok descent . He received his Bachelors in Computer Science at Humboldt State University. After his bachelors he chose to go to University of California, Davis, for his Masters in Computer Science. Immediately after graduating he came to Google and has been there for the last 7 months.

Joanna Milner

Joanna Milner serves as the Google American Indian Network's Chief Operating Officer. Originally from the Bay Area, she holds a B.A. from Washington University in St. Louis in Art History & Archeology.
